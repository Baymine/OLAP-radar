## 2. Paper Snapshot

- **Title:** _Bespoke OLAP: Synthesizing Workload-Specific One-size-fits-one Database Engines_  
- **Authors:** Johannes Wehrstein, Timo Eckmann, Matthias Jasny, Carsten Binnig  
- **Publication date:** 2026-03-02  
- **Research problem:** Modern OLAP systems pay unavoidable overheads for generality, including schema interpretation, indirection, and abstraction layers, even when workloads are fixed or highly predictable. Building a custom engine for a known workload could remove these costs, but has historically required prohibitive engineering effort.  
- **Core method:** The paper presents a fully autonomous synthesis pipeline that uses LLM-based code generation combined with iterative performance evaluation and automated validation to produce a workload-specific database engine. The system refines decisions across both storage layout and query execution, aiming to generate a correct and high-performance specialized engine within minutes to hours.

## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

This paper is highly relevant to **query engine design** because it shifts optimization from plan selection inside a fixed engine to **engine synthesis itself**. For recurring analytical workloads, dashboards, and embedded BI scenarios, a bespoke engine could outperform even highly optimized vectorized and columnar systems by removing layers of runtime generality. It also matters for **storage and lakehouse-adjacent architectures**, since workload-specific physical design and execution code can be co-generated rather than tuned separately. More broadly, the paper raises an important research question for OLAP: whether future performance breakthroughs come less from better universal optimizers and more from automated specialization pipelines.
