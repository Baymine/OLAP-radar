## 2. Paper Snapshot

- **Authors:** Jiale Lao, Immanuel Trummer

- **Research Problem:** Traditional OLAP engines are difficult to extend and expensive to engineer, while rapidly changing hardware, workloads, and user requirements make static engine design increasingly rigid.  
- **Core Method:** GenDB uses an LLM-powered multi-agent system to synthesize customized execution code per query, generating instance-optimized implementations rather than relying on a fixed database engine. The prototype is evaluated on TPC-H and a newly constructed benchmark intended to reduce benchmark contamination from LLM training data.

## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

For OLAP systems, the central question is whether one-size-fits-all execution engines are reaching diminishing returns relative to workload-specialized code generation. GenDB suggests that future analytical systems may shift from maintaining increasingly complex vectorized/compiled execution stacks toward synthesizing bespoke operators, pipelines, or even end-to-end execution plans on demand. That has direct relevance to **query optimization**, since costing may evolve from operator selection to synthesis-guided plan generation, and to **benchmarking**, since standard suites like TPC-H may no longer be sufficient when systems can overfit at the code level. The paper also resonates with lakehouse and cloud analytics trends, where hardware heterogeneity and workload diversity make adaptive specialization especially attractive.
