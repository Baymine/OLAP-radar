The clearest signal today is that **core OLAP and data-infrastructure projects are not winning broad GitHub trending attention**, even while the ecosystem itself remains structurally healthy. Community energy on the main trending page is overwhelmingly captured by AI-agent tooling, which suggests data infrastructure is currently in a phase of **deep platform construction rather than short-term hype spikes**. Within the filtered set, the strongest attention centers on **mature analytical engines**—especially [ClickHouse](https://github.com/ClickHouse/ClickHouse), [DuckDB](https://github.com/duckdb/duckdb), [Apache Doris](https://github.com/apache/doris), and [StarRocks](https://github.com/StarRocks/starrocks). These represent the continuing consolidation of fast SQL analytics around columnar execution, embedded OLAP, and hybrid lake/warehouse query models.

A second important signal is the rise of the **lakehouse control plane** as a standalone category. Projects such as [Apache Polaris](https://github.com/apache/polaris), [Apache Gravitino](https://github.com/apache/gravitino), and [Lakekeeper](https://github.com/lakekeeper/lakekeeper) show that metadata, catalog interoperability, and governance are becoming as strategically important as storage itself. Meanwhile, [Apache Fluss](https://github.com/apache/fluss) and [OLake](https://github.com/datazip-inc/olake) point to a convergence between streaming ingestion and lakehouse-native storage.

On the architectural side, **Rust remains a major implementation trend** across compute and metadata infrastructure: [DataFusion](https://github.com/apache/datafusion), [Databend](https://github.com/databendlabs/databend), and [Lakekeeper](https://github.com/lakekeeper/lakekeeper) reinforce this. New directions also include **embedded and browser-side analytics** via [chDB](https://github.com/chdb-io/chdb) and [DuckDB-Wasm](https://github.com/duckdb/duckdb-wasm), connecting directly to recent cloud-warehouse pressure for lower-latency, lower-cost, and more portable analytical execution.
