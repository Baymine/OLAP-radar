## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

For OLAP practitioners, this paper is interesting because it challenges a foundational assumption: that one engine must efficiently serve many workloads. In columnar analytics, much of the remaining overhead comes from runtime schema interpretation, indirection, and generic execution abstractions; a synthesized engine specialized to a known workload can potentially remove these layers entirely. This has direct relevance to query engine architecture, physical design, code generation, and benchmarking, especially for stable enterprise dashboards, embedded analytics, and repeated analytical pipelines. It also raises a broader systems question for lakehouse and warehouse platforms: whether future optimizers should stop at plan generation, or go further and synthesize the execution substrate itself.
