## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

This paper goes straight at one of the biggest long-term questions in analytical data infrastructure: how much of an OLAP engine should be hand-engineered versus synthesized per workload. For data warehouse, lakehouse, and embedded analytics settings with stable dashboards, repeated query templates, or domain-specific schemas, a one-size-fits-one engine could remove runtime indirection, schema interpretation, and generic operator overhead. It is especially relevant to query optimization and benchmarking because it suggests a future where benchmarks are not only used to compare engines, but to generate them. For researchers, it raises new questions around correctness guarantees, maintainability, workload drift, and whether physical design, code generation, and engine synthesis should converge into a single automated stack.
