## 2. Paper Snapshot

- **Title:** GenDB: The Next Generation of Query Processing -- Synthesized, Not Engineered  
- **Authors:** Jiale Lao, Immanuel Trummer  
- **Publication date:** 2026-03-02  
- **Research problem:** Traditional OLAP/query engines are expensive to build and maintain, hard to extend, and slow to adapt to new hardware, workloads, and optimization opportunities.  
- **Core method:** GenDB uses an LLM-powered multi-agent synthesis pipeline to generate instance-optimized execution code for each incoming query rather than relying on a prebuilt general-purpose engine. The system evaluates on OLAP workloads including TPC-H and a new benchmark intended to reduce training-data leakage concerns.

## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

This paper goes straight at the heart of OLAP infrastructure: the design of the query engine itself. If per-query synthesis can reliably produce faster execution than mature columnar engines, it suggests a new path beyond traditional optimizer + execution engine architectures, especially for analytical workloads with recurring structure or deployable specialization opportunities. The idea is also highly relevant to lakehouse and warehouse systems, where heterogeneous hardware, mixed storage layouts, and rapidly evolving semantics make static engine design increasingly costly. For researchers, it raises major questions around benchmarking, correctness, synthesis latency, reproducibility, and whether “engine generation” becomes a new optimization layer.
