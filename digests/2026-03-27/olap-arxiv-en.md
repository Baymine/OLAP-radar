## 3. Why It Matters for OLAP

For OLAP practitioners, the paper is significant because it pushes beyond traditional optimizer work toward **engine synthesis as an optimization strategy**. Instead of choosing among existing operators and plans, GenDB effectively generates new execution implementations on demand, which could eliminate abstraction overheads common in general-purpose analytical engines. This has implications for **query compilation**, **vectorized execution**, **hardware specialization**, and potentially even **storage-layout-aware code generation** in warehouse and lakehouse environments.

The paper is also important from a benchmarking and systems perspective. It compares against strong analytical baselines, including DuckDB and ClickHouse, and introduces a benchmark motivated by concerns about LLM memorization of standard workloads. Even if the approach proves difficult to operationalize in production, it may reshape how the community thinks about extensibility, engine architecture, and the boundary between optimizer and runtime in analytical systems.
