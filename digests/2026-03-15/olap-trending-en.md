## 3. Trend Signal Analysis

Today’s strongest community attention is going to **AI-adjacent data infrastructure**, especially systems that combine retrieval, indexing, and analytics-oriented storage. The clearest immediate signal is [OpenRAG](https://github.com/langflow-ai/openrag), which stands out on the daily trending list with significant same-day star growth. While not a traditional OLAP engine, it reflects where fresh developer energy is moving: data systems that prepare, index, and serve context for AI workloads using familiar search and pipeline components.

At the same time, the broader repository set shows that **core OLAP remains highly stable and consolidated** around [ClickHouse](https://github.com/ClickHouse/ClickHouse), [DuckDB](https://github.com/duckdb/duckdb), [Apache Doris](https://github.com/apache/doris), [StarRocks](https://github.com/StarRocks/starrocks), and [Trino](https://github.com/trinodb/trino). These are no longer just databases; they are becoming platforms around embedded execution, lakehouse interoperability, and mixed real-time/offline analytics.

On the innovation side, **new storage and execution directions are visible**. [facebookincubator/nimble](https://github.com/facebookincubator/nimble) is one of the more interesting format-level signals, suggesting ongoing experimentation beyond Parquet-centric orthodoxy. Meanwhile, [Apache Polaris](https://github.com/apache/polaris), [Apache Gravitino](https://github.com/apache/gravitino), and [Lakekeeper](https://github.com/lakekeeper/lakekeeper) confirm that the catalog/metadata control plane is now a first-class battleground in lakehouse architecture.

A final pattern is the rise of **modular, Rust-heavy compute layers** such as [DataFusion](https://github.com/apache/datafusion), [Databend](https://github.com/databendlabs/databend), and [Lakekeeper](https://github.com/lakekeeper/lakekeeper). This aligns closely with recent cloud-warehouse and lakehouse developments: object storage as the system of record, open table/catalog standards for interoperability, and lightweight embedded or composable engines for specialized workloads.
